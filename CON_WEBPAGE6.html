College of Nursing (CON)

The College of Nursing is dedicated to shaping compassionate, competent, and service-oriented healthcare professionals who uphold the highest standards of nursing practice. It provides a strong foundation in medical science, clinical skills, and ethical care through a balance of classroom instruction and hands-on experience in hospitals and community settings. Guided by a commitment to excellence, integrity, and social responsibility, the college prepares students to respond effectively to the evolving needs of the healthcare industry.


The College of Nursing at Lyceum of the Philippines University – Cavite is committed to producing compassionate, competent, and service-oriented healthcare professionals who exemplify the highest standards of nursing practice. The college’s programs are designed to provide students with a robust foundation in medical science, clinical skills, patient care, and ethical practice. By combining rigorous classroom instruction with hands-on clinical experience in hospitals, healthcare facilities, and community settings, the college ensures that students are well-prepared to meet the demands of an ever-evolving healthcare landscape.

At the heart of the College of Nursing’s mission is the cultivation of critical thinking, problem-solving abilities, and social responsibility. Students are trained not only to diagnose and provide care but also to assess complex patient needs, make informed decisions under pressure, and implement evidence-based interventions that promote holistic health and well-being. Emphasis is placed on professional ethics, empathy, and compassionate care, ensuring that graduates are not only technically proficient but also humanistic and culturally sensitive healthcare providers.

A significant aspect of the College of Nursing experience is its vibrant student organization community, which complements formal education by fostering leadership, collaboration, and personal growth. These organizations provide students with opportunities to develop soft skills, network with peers and professionals, participate in community service, and engage in professional development activities. Active involvement in student organizations also encourages teamwork, accountability, and the development of organizational and management skills, which are critical for future nursing leaders.
