College of Medical Allied Sciences (CAMS)

The College of Allied Medical Sciences (CAMS) is committed to developing competent, ethical, and compassionate professionals in the field of health and medical sciences. The college provides a strong foundation in scientific knowledge, clinical skills, and research, preparing students to excel in various healthcare disciplines. Through hands-on laboratory training, community immersion, and partnerships with medical institutions, CAMS ensures that its graduates are well-equipped to contribute to the promotion of health, prevention of disease, and improvement of patient care.


The College of Allied Medical Sciences (CAMS) is dedicated not only to providing a rigorous academic and clinical education but also to fostering a vibrant community of student organizations that play a critical role in shaping well-rounded healthcare professionals. These organizations are designed to complement the formal curriculum by offering students opportunities to engage in activities that enhance leadership, teamwork, and professional development, all within a context of service, ethics, and compassionate care. By joining CAMS student organizations, students gain hands-on experience in health-related projects, public health campaigns, research initiatives, and community outreach programs, allowing them to translate classroom and laboratory learning into meaningful real-world impact. CAMS student organizations also serve as platforms for interdisciplinary collaboration, enabling students from various health-related programs—such as medical technology, radiologic technology, physical therapy, nutrition, and other allied health disciplines—to work together. This collaboration fosters a holistic understanding of patient care and health systems, reflecting the real-world environment of modern healthcare, where teamwork among diverse professionals is essential for effective service delivery.

In addition to enhancing clinical and scientific competencies, these organizations emphasize the development of essential soft skills, including communication, problem-solving, time management, and ethical decision-making. Students frequently take on leadership roles, organize seminars, workshops, and community health initiatives, or lead research and advocacy projects, all of which cultivate a sense of initiative, responsibility, and professionalism. Furthermore, involvement in these groups often provides mentorship opportunities, connecting students with faculty members and healthcare practitioners who guide their career development, encourage innovation, and support scholarly pursuits. Overall, student organizations within the College of Allied Medical Sciences play an integral role in bridging academic learning with professional practice. They provide a dynamic environment where students can grow intellectually, professionally, and personally, equipping them with the knowledge, skills, and values necessary to excel in the healthcare field. By fostering leadership, collaboration, ethical conduct, and community engagement, CAMS student organizations ensure that graduates are not only competent and skilled but also compassionate and socially responsible professionals ready to make meaningful contributions to the promotion of health, the prevention of disease, and the improvement of patient care.
