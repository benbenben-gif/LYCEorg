College of Tourism and Hospitality Management (CITHM)

The College of Tourism and Hospitality Management is committed to nurturing globally competitive professionals equipped with the skills, values, and passion to excel in the dynamic fields of tourism, hospitality, and travel. The college provides a holistic learning environment that combines academic excellence, hands-on training, and industry exposure to prepare students for real-world experiences in hotels, restaurants, airlines, and event management.


The College of Tourism and Hospitality Management (CTHM) at Lyceum of the Philippines University–Cavite is home to a diverse array of student organizations that play a vital role in shaping future leaders in the tourism, hospitality, and travel industries. These organizations provide students with opportunities to complement their academic learning through hands-on experiences, professional development, and collaborative projects that mirror the dynamic and fast-paced nature of the global hospitality sector. By participating in these organizations, students are able to apply theoretical knowledge from the classroom to real-world scenarios such as hotel operations, restaurant management, event planning, airline services, and tourism development initiatives. CTHM student organizations cultivate essential leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills, enabling students to manage teams, coordinate events, and interact effectively with both peers and industry professionals. Students often take on roles that involve organizing hospitality events, tourism promotions, or industry workshops, which enhances their project management, problem-solving, and decision-making abilities. These experiences not only build confidence but also prepare students to adapt to the challenges of a dynamic industry where service excellence, attention to detail, and customer satisfaction are paramount.

Beyond professional development, student organizations in CTHM foster creativity, innovation, and cultural appreciation. Many groups engage in tourism advocacy, community-based tourism projects, sustainability initiatives, and hospitality outreach programs that allow students to promote local culture, heritage, and responsible tourism practices. These activities instill a strong sense of social responsibility, ethical practice, and civic-mindedness, emphasizing that tourism and hospitality are not only about service but also about positively impacting communities and preserving cultural and environmental resources. CTHM student organizations also provide platforms for networking, mentorship, and industry exposure. By collaborating with faculty advisors, alumni, and hospitality professionals, students gain insights into emerging trends, best practices, and career opportunities in hotels, resorts, cruise lines, airlines, event management, and tourism enterprises. Participation in competitions, internships, and industry partnerships further enhances students’ practical skills, preparing them to transition smoothly from academic study to professional careers.
